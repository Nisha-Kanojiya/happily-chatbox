Material wellbeing has been improving worldwide over the last several decades. We are all after money, physical fitness and great looks in the pursuit of happiness.

But our minds are becoming weaker, and unhappiness is increasing rapidly.

The world is experiencing a mental health crisis. Anger, violence, stress, anxiety, depression, addictions, and many other mental illnesses are increasing world-wide.
In the US alone, we spend approximately $80-130bn annually on treating mental illnesses, and another $160 billion on religious, spiritual, and coaching services, and products!
About 60 million people need help, but only half get it. We are only 4.3% of world population, but consume 66% of mental health drugs.
Our nation’s youth are being seriously affected by challenges at school, home and work. The COVID pandemic, racial and gender discrimination, and school shootings, have worsened the situation. Psychological problems have a stigma.

Life is full of ups and downs. Our minds (emotions and feelings) control how we overcome hurdles, thrive and enjoy life.

Strengthening and healing our minds is essential for happiness. Sharing our stories with others, and seeing how they too are facing hurdles and finding solutions, can help us find our own solutions.
